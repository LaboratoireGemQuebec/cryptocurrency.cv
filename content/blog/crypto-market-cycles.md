Bitcoin has completed four full market cycles since its creation in 2009. Each cycle follows a remarkably similar pattern: a slow accumulation phase, a dramatic bull run, a peak and euphoric distribution, and a painful bear market that resets the cycle. Understanding these cycles does not guarantee perfect timing — but it gives you a framework for making decisions at different market stages.

This guide explains the four phases of crypto market cycles, their relationship to Bitcoin halvings, and how to identify which phase you are in. For real-time market analysis, follow [Crypto Vision News](/).

## Why Crypto Has 4-Year Cycles

The primary driver of Bitcoin's market cycles is the halving — a programmatic event embedded in Bitcoin's code that cuts the mining reward in half approximately every four years (every 210,000 blocks).

**Bitcoin halving schedule:**
- 2012: 25 BTC per block (was 50)
- 2016: 12.5 BTC per block
- 2020: 6.25 BTC per block
- 2024: 3.125 BTC per block
- 2028: 1.5625 BTC per block (projected)

Each halving reduces Bitcoin's supply issuance by 50%. If demand stays constant and supply shrinks, price rises. This supply shock is the mechanical foundation of the bull market that typically follows each halving by 12–18 months.

## The Four Phases of a Crypto Market Cycle

### Phase 1: Accumulation (Bear Market Bottom)

**Characteristics:**
- Price is 70–85% below the previous all-time high
- Trading volume is low; media coverage is minimal or negative
- "Bitcoin is dead" articles appear regularly
- Long-term holders (HODLers) are absorbing supply from panic sellers
- Sentiment: Despair → Disbelief

**On-chain signals:**
- MVRV ratio below 1 (market cap below realized cap — holders are underwater on average)
- Exchange outflows (Bitcoin leaving exchanges into cold storage)
- Low funding rates or negative funding on futures
- SOPR below 1 (people selling at a loss)

**Historical examples:** 2015 (post-Mt.Gox), 2019, late 2022 (post-FTX)

### Phase 2: Markup (Early to Middle Bull Market)

**Characteristics:**
- Price begins recovering, often slowly at first
- Halving event occurs or is recent history
- Early adopters and institutional accumulation begins
- Media coverage turns cautiously positive
- Altcoins begin outperforming Bitcoin
- Sentiment: Hope → Optimism → Belief

**On-chain signals:**
- MVRV ratio rising from 1 toward 2–3
- Exchange inflows begin (less, but increasing)
- Positive funding rates returning
- SOPR above 1 (most holders profitable)
- Spot ETF inflows increasing (in recent cycles)

**Historical examples:** 2016–2017, 2020–2021, 2023–2024

### Phase 3: Distribution (Late Bull Market Peak)

**Characteristics:**
- Price at or near all-time highs
- Mainstream media coverage is intense and positive
- Your family, coworkers, and taxi drivers ask about crypto
- FOMO is everywhere; rational analysis is dismissed
- Altcoins in parabolics, memecoins making headlines
- New retail investors entering at the top
- Sentiment: Euphoria → Mania

**On-chain signals:**
- MVRV ratio above 3.5 (historically near peaks)
- Heavy exchange inflows (long-term holders distributing to new buyers)
- Extremely high positive funding rates
- Rising long-term holder selling (LTH-SOPR spikes)
- Stablecoin supply declining (converted to crypto = buying pressure)

**Historical examples:** December 2017, November 2021

### Phase 4: Markdown (Bear Market)

**Characteristics:**
- Price declining from highs; initial drops dismissed as "corrections"
- Leveraged positions getting liquidated in cascades
- High-profile failures: over-leveraged funds, centralized lenders, Ponzi schemes exposed
- Media coverage turns negative
- "This time is different — crypto is dead" narrative
- Sentiment: Anxiety → Denial → Capitulation

**On-chain signals:**
- MVRV declining sharply toward or below 1
- Massive exchange inflows (panic selling)
- Declining active addresses
- Crypto-correlated company failures (exchanges, funds, lenders)

**Historical examples:** 2018 (-84%), 2022 (-77% with FTX, Celsius, Three Arrows Capital collapses)

## Key Cycle Indicators to Track

### MVRV Ratio (Market Value to Realized Value)

Compares market cap to realized cap (what all holders paid on average). MVRV above 3.5 has historically signaled overvaluation. Below 1 signals undervaluation. Available on Glassnode.

### Bitcoin Dominance

BTC dominance (BTC market cap / total crypto market cap) typically peaks during bear markets and bottoms during altcoin season. Falling dominance often signals peak altcoin activity.

### Fear & Greed Index

A composite sentiment indicator. Extreme greed (80–100) historically correlates with market peaks. Extreme fear (0–20) correlates with buying opportunities. Monitor at alternative.me.

### Funding Rates

Average perpetual futures funding rates. Sustained high positive rates (>0.05% per 8 hours) signal overheating. Negative rates signal fear.

### Altcoin Season Index

Measures what percentage of top-100 altcoins have outperformed Bitcoin over the past 90 days. Above 75 = altcoin season. Below 25 = Bitcoin season.

## How to Use Cycle Knowledge Strategically

1. **Accumulate during Phase 1** — the hardest time emotionally, the best time mathematically
2. **Hold through Phase 2** — the trend is your friend; resist taking profits too early
3. **Begin reducing risk in Phase 3** — use on-chain indicators to identify distribution; take partial profits
4. **Hold stablecoins through Phase 4** — preserve capital for the next accumulation phase

No one rings a bell at the top or bottom. But understanding cycles helps you maintain perspective when mainstream narratives are screaming "this time is different."
