The collapse of FTX in November 2022 was a watershed moment: billions of dollars of customer funds, supposedly held safely on a trusted centralized exchange, simply vanished due to fraud. "Not your keys, not your coins" went from a DeFi slogan to lived reality for hundreds of thousands of users.

Yet CeFi — centralized finance — has not disappeared. Coinbase went public on NASDAQ. Binance processes hundreds of billions in monthly volume. The question is not which model "wins," but when to use each. This guide breaks down the real differences. For DeFi and market news, visit [Crypto Vision News](/).

## Defining DeFi and CeFi

**CeFi (Centralized Finance)** includes any crypto service operated by a centralized company:
- Centralized exchanges (Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, Bybit)
- Centralized lending (Genesis, BlockFi — both failed in 2022)
- Centralized custody (Coinbase Custody, BitGo)
- CEX staking and savings products

**DeFi (Decentralized Finance)** operates through open-source smart contracts on public blockchains:
- Decentralized exchanges (Uniswap, Curve)
- Lending protocols (Aave, Compound)
- Stablecoins (DAI/USDS, LUSD)
- Yield aggregators (Yearn, Beefy)
- No central company controls user funds

## Key Differences

### Custody

**CeFi:** The exchange holds your private keys. You have an account with a balance — similar to a bank account. This is simple and familiar, but it means the exchange can freeze your account, block withdrawals, or lose your funds.

**DeFi:** You hold your private keys. Your wallet directly holds tokens. No protocol can block your transaction or freeze your funds (though smart contracts can have bugs).

**Lesson from FTX:** FTX held customer deposits in a sister trading firm's accounts and lost billions to bad bets. When withdrawals were requested, funds were not there. DeFi protocols cannot do this — the code is transparent and funds cannot be secretly moved.

### KYC and Anonymity

**CeFi:** KYC (Know Your Customer) is mandatory. You must submit government ID, proof of address, sometimes selfies and source-of-funds documentation. This creates compliance but also creates data breach risk and restricts access for users in certain countries.

**DeFi:** No KYC. Interact with any protocol using just a wallet address. Some DeFi frontends now implement geo-blocking or require attestations, but underlying smart contracts remain permissionless.

### Transparency

**CeFi:** Balance sheets are opaque. Users trusted FTX based on reputation and regulatory licensing — not because they could verify the reserves.

**DeFi:** Fully transparent. Every transaction, every reserve, every interest rate change is verifiable onchain by anyone. Protocol solvency can be audited in real time through tools like DeFiLlama.

### User Experience

**CeFi:** Far easier for newcomers. Create an account, buy with a credit card or bank transfer, see a familiar balance screen. Customer support exists. Password recovery exists. Mobile apps are polished.

**DeFi:** Steeper learning curve. Requires understanding wallets, seed phrases, gas fees, transaction confirmations, and interacting with smart contracts. Errors can be irreversible — there is no customer support to call.

### Fees

**CeFi:** Trading fees typically 0.1–0.5% for spot trades; withdrawal fees vary. Hidden spreads on retail conversions can be significant.

**DeFi:** Smart contract protocol fees typically 0.01–0.3% for swaps; gas fees on top (variable — can be $0.01 on L2 or $20+ on Ethereum mainnet during congestion).

### Regulatory Status

**CeFi:** Regulated (or seeking regulation) in most major jurisdictions. Coinbase is a publicly traded company with SEC reporting. This provides legal protections and enables fiat on/off ramps.

**DeFi:** Regulatory status varies. Some jurisdictions are applying securities laws to DeFi protocols. Frontend operators face legal risk in some countries. The underlying smart contracts are generally not legal entities.

## Pros and Cons Summary

| Factor | CeFi | DeFi |
|--------|------|------|
| Custody | Exchange controls | You control |
| KYC | Required | Not required |
| Transparency | Opaque | Fully transparent |
| UX | Easy | Complex |
| Fiat access | Direct | Via bridges |
| Insurance | Sometimes (FDIC does not cover crypto) | Smart contract cover via Nexus Mutual |
| Risk | Counterparty, fraud | Smart contract, liquidation |
| Recovery | Password reset possible | Seed phrase only — no recovery |

## When to Use CeFi

**Choose CeFi when:**
- Buying crypto with fiat (bank transfer, credit card)
- Converting large amounts without worrying about gas fees
- Accessing products unavailable in DeFi (regulated futures, institutional custody)
- You are new to crypto and learning
- Compliance records matter for tax purposes
- High-volume trading where CeFi offers better price discovery

**Best CeFi platforms in 2026:** Coinbase, Kraken (most regulated), Binance (highest volume), Bybit (derivatives focus)

## When to Use DeFi

**Choose DeFi when:**
- Self-custody is your priority ("not your keys, not your coins")
- Accessing yields not available on CEXs
- Trading tokens not listed on exchanges
- Privacy is important
- You want transparent, verifiable protocol rules
- Using advanced strategies (flash loans, LP provision, leveraged farming)

## The FTX Collapse: DeFi's Moment of Validation

FTX was the second-largest exchange globally, trusted by millions, backed by prominent venture capitalists. When it collapsed in November 2022, approximately $8 billion in customer funds were lost.

What happened? FTX secretly lent customer funds to its sister trading firm Alameda Research, which used them as collateral for risky bets. When the bets failed and withdrawals were requested, the funds were not there.

**In DeFi, this is impossible by design.** Aave and Compound cannot secretly move user funds. Every reserve is visible on-chain. Liquidations are automated and transparent. The FTX collapse drove hundreds of thousands of users toward self-custody and DeFi for exactly this reason.

## Hybrid Approaches

Most sophisticated crypto users operate in both worlds:

1. Use Coinbase or Kraken to buy ETH/BTC with fiat
2. Withdraw to a hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor)
3. Use MetaMask or similar to interact with DeFi protocols
4. Keep only trading amounts on exchanges; hold savings in self-custody

This captures CeFi's convenience for fiat transactions while maintaining DeFi's self-custody principles for long-term holdings.

The DeFi vs CeFi debate is ultimately not binary — it is about matching the right tool to the right task, with clear eyes about the risks of each.
