Creating and selling an NFT is more accessible than most people expect. You don't need coding knowledge, and you can go from idea to listed NFT in under an hour. This guide walks through every step for 2026, covering the current platforms and best practices.

## Before You Start: Key Decisions

### Choose Your Blockchain

The blockchain you choose affects fees, audience, and compatible marketplaces.

**Ethereum:** The largest NFT market, home to the most established collections and highest-value sales. Gas fees can be significant for frequent transactions, but Layer 2 options reduce costs. Best for serious art and established collectors.

**Base (Coinbase's L2):** Growing rapidly in 2025-2026 for creators. Very low fees, Ethereum security, and access to Coinbase's user base. Good for new creators.

**Solana:** Fast, cheap transactions and an active NFT community. Magic Eden is the dominant marketplace. Good for gaming NFTs and high-frequency trading.

**Polygon:** Low fees, Ethereum-compatible, used by brands and mainstream audiences. OpenSea supports Polygon.

For most new creators, **Base or Polygon** offers the best balance of low costs and broad marketplace support.

### Choose Your File Type

NFTs can represent:
- Images (PNG, JPG, GIF, SVG)
- Video (MP4, MOV)
- Audio (MP3, WAV)
- 3D models (GLB, GLTF)
- Interactive files (HTML)

Most marketplaces support standard image formats. Video and interactive NFTs have higher minting requirements on some platforms.

## Step 1: Set Up a Crypto Wallet

You need a self-custody wallet to hold your NFTs and receive payments.

**For Ethereum/Base/Polygon:** Install MetaMask at metamask.io (browser extension or mobile app). Create a new wallet and securely back up your 12-word seed phrase.

**For Solana:** Install Phantom at phantom.app.

Fund your wallet with the native token of your chosen chain:
- Ethereum → ETH
- Base → ETH (bridged to Base)
- Polygon → MATIC
- Solana → SOL

You need a small amount for gas fees (transactions). On Layer 2 chains, fees are typically under $0.01. On Ethereum mainnet, fees vary — check current rates at ethgasstation.info.

## Step 2: Prepare Your Digital Asset

Your NFT starts as a digital file. Quality matters more in 2026 — the speculative era is over and collectors are more discerning.

**Technical preparation:**
- Images: minimum 2000x2000 pixels for art NFTs; transparent background for collectibles
- Maximum file sizes vary by platform — typically 100MB
- Create multiple resolutions if possible (thumbnail, full resolution)

**Metadata planning:**
- Name: the title of your NFT
- Description: detailed description of the work, process, or concept
- Attributes/Traits: properties that define variants (if creating a collection)

For collections of many items, metadata is typically prepared as JSON files and images in bulk. Single 1-of-1 art pieces can be created individually on the marketplace UI.

## Step 3: Choose a Marketplace

### OpenSea (opensea.io)
The largest multi-chain NFT marketplace. Supports Ethereum, Base, Polygon, Arbitrum, and others. Good for broad visibility. Charges a 2.5% service fee on sales.

**Creating on OpenSea:**
1. Go to opensea.io and connect your wallet
2. Click your profile → Create
3. First create a Collection (even for single pieces, you need a collection)
4. Set collection details: name, description, image, category
5. Add your NFT within the collection

### Blur (blur.io)
Ethereum-focused, popular among active traders. Better for collections and secondary trading than single pieces. Lower fees than OpenSea.

### Foundation (foundation.app)
Curated platform for digital artists. Higher barrier to entry (invite-based historically), but connects with serious art collectors. 5% service fee. Best for 1-of-1 fine art.

### Zora (zora.co)
Open platform supporting Base and Ethereum. Creator-friendly with free minting options and the ability to create open editions (unlimited prints at a price). Great for musicians and artists building community.

### Magic Eden (magiceden.io)
Best choice for Solana NFTs. Also supports Ethereum and Bitcoin Ordinals.

## Step 4: Mint Your NFT

Using OpenSea as an example (the most common starting point):

1. **Connect wallet** to opensea.io
2. **Create Collection:** Profile → My Collections → Create a Collection
   - Upload banner, logo, and featured images
   - Set collection name, description, and category
   - Set creator royalty percentage (0-10% recommended)
   - Select blockchain
3. **Add NFT:** Within your collection, click "Add Item"
   - Upload your file
   - Add name, description, and external link
   - Add properties/traits if applicable
   - Set supply (1 for unique, higher for editions)
4. **Confirm creation** in your MetaMask wallet (sign the transaction)

On many Layer 2 chains, minting is free — you only pay when it sells. On Ethereum mainnet, minting typically costs $5-50+ in gas depending on network conditions.

## Step 5: Set Royalties

Royalties let you earn a percentage of every future sale on secondary markets. Set them during collection creation.

**Recommended range:** 2.5% to 10%
- High-profile art collections often use 5-10%
- Gaming items typically 2.5-5%
- Commercial/brand NFTs may use lower rates

**Reality check in 2026:** Many marketplaces (Blur, X2Y2) don't enforce royalties. OpenSea and Foundation do. When setting royalties, consider that enforcement varies by platform.

## Step 6: List for Sale

After minting, your NFT is in your wallet but not yet for sale. To list it:

1. Go to your NFT's page on the marketplace
2. Click "Sell" or "List"
3. Choose between:
   - **Fixed price:** Set a specific price and duration
   - **Auction:** Set a starting price and duration
4. Confirm the listing transaction in your wallet

## Promoting Your NFT

Minting is only half the work — getting it seen requires promotion:

- **Twitter/X:** Share the work with relevant hashtags (#NFT, #CryptoArt, #Web3Art)
- **Discord:** Join collector and creator communities; participate before promoting
- **Farcaster:** Web3-native social platform increasingly important for NFT creators
- **Newsletters:** Platforms like Mirror let you build an audience and announce drops

## Costs to Expect

- **Wallet setup:** Free
- **Gas for minting:** Free on Layer 2s; $5-50 on Ethereum mainnet
- **Marketplace fee on sale:** Typically 2.5% (OpenSea) deducted automatically
- **Creator royalty to yourself:** Goes to your wallet when others buy/resell

## Tips for First-Time NFT Creators

1. Start on a low-fee chain to learn the process without significant costs
2. Mint one test NFT before doing a full collection
3. Build community before launch — audiences don't appear overnight
4. Quality over quantity — one great piece outperforms ten mediocre ones
5. Set realistic pricing based on comparable works, not aspirational targets

The NFT market in 2026 rewards genuine creativity and community-building. The path from creation to sale has never been more technically accessible.
