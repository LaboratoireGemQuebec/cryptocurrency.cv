Tax-loss harvesting is one of the few legal strategies that can significantly reduce your crypto tax bill — and it is available to anyone who has unrealized losses in their portfolio. Yet most crypto holders ignore it, leaving thousands of dollars in potential tax savings on the table.

The strategy is simple: sell losing positions to realize capital losses, then use those losses to offset gains. In crypto, this is especially powerful because of a key difference from stock markets. This guide shows you exactly how to do it.

## What Is Tax-Loss Harvesting?

Tax-loss harvesting means deliberately selling assets at a loss to realize capital losses that can offset taxable gains.

**Example:**
- You sold Bitcoin for a $20,000 capital gain in March
- You also hold ETH purchased at $4,000 that is currently worth $2,500 (unrealized loss: $1,500)
- If you sell the ETH, you realize a $1,500 capital loss
- Net taxable gain: $20,000 - $1,500 = $18,500

If you are in the 20% long-term capital gains bracket, this saves $300 in taxes on that loss alone. Scale this across a diversified portfolio with multiple losing positions and the savings compound significantly.

## The Capital Loss Rules

In the U.S.:
- Capital losses first offset capital gains of the same type (long-term losses → long-term gains, short-term losses → short-term gains)
- If losses exceed gains, they can offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year
- Remaining losses carry forward indefinitely to future tax years

**Example of carry-forward value:**
- 2025: $10,000 in realized losses, $0 in gains
- Apply $3,000 against ordinary income → saves ~$720 at 24% bracket
- Carry forward $7,000 to 2026
- 2026: $7,000 in gains — completely offset by carried-forward losses → saves $1,400 at 20% LTCG rate

A $10,000 realized loss in 2025 generates $2,120 in total tax savings over two years.

## The Wash Sale Rule: The Critical Crypto Difference

In stock markets, the IRS wash sale rule prevents you from claiming a capital loss if you repurchase the "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale.

**For cryptocurrency:** As of 2026, there is no wash sale rule for crypto assets under current U.S. tax law. Cryptocurrency is classified as property, not a security, so wash sale restrictions do not apply.

**What this means:** You can sell Bitcoin at a loss and immediately buy it back. You crystallize the tax loss while maintaining your BTC position and cost basis reset.

**Example:**
- You hold 0.5 BTC purchased at $90,000 (cost basis: $45,000)
- Current BTC price: $75,000 (current value: $37,500)
- Unrealized loss: $7,500

**Tax-loss harvest:**
1. Sell 0.5 BTC at $75,000 → realize $7,500 capital loss
2. Immediately buy 0.5 BTC at $75,000 → new cost basis $37,500
3. You still own 0.5 BTC; your loss is realized for tax purposes
4. If BTC later rises to $100,000, your taxable gain will be calculated from the new $75,000 basis

**Warning:** Congressional proposals to apply wash sale rules to crypto have been discussed since 2021. Check current law before executing any strategy, as this could change.

## Year-End Harvesting Strategy

Tax-loss harvesting is most valuable at year-end (October–December) when you can see your full picture of realized gains for the year.

**Step-by-step year-end process:**

1. **Calculate your realized gains for the year** — use tax software (Koinly, CoinTracker) to see your current taxable position
2. **Identify unrealized losses** — scan your portfolio for positions with meaningful losses
3. **Prioritize short-term losses first** — they offset short-term gains taxed at ordinary income rates (higher value)
4. **Calculate potential tax savings** — for each losing position, calculate: loss amount × your marginal rate
5. **Execute before December 31** — losses must be realized in the calendar year to count
6. **Repurchase immediately** (if desired) to maintain exposure

## What to Harvest: Prioritization Framework

Not all losses are worth harvesting. Consider:

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Short-term loss, you pay 37% ordinary income rate | High priority — harvest immediately |
| Long-term loss, you have significant LT gains this year | High priority |
| Small loss (<$500), high gas fees | May not be worthwhile after transaction costs |
| Loss in asset you plan to sell soon anyway | Harvest now to set the timing |
| Loss on an asset you believe in | Harvest and immediately repurchase |

## Multi-Year Strategy Example

**Year 1 (Bear Market):**
- Sell BTC with $30,000 loss
- Sell SOL with $15,000 loss
- Total realized losses: $45,000
- Offset $3,000 against ordinary income; carry forward $42,000

**Year 2 (Early Recovery):**
- BTC rises; you sell for $20,000 gain
- Carried losses offset: $42,000 - $20,000 = $22,000 remaining carry-forward
- Tax on gains: $0

**Year 3 (Bull Market):**
- Sell ETH for $35,000 gain
- Carried losses offset $22,000 → taxable gain is $13,000
- Tax saving from $22,000 carry-forward: $4,400 at 20% LTCG rate

Starting from a $45,000 loss position, you saved taxes for three years and deferred/eliminated substantial tax obligations.

## Common Tax-Loss Harvesting Mistakes

### 1. Waiting Until December 31

Prices can move rapidly. Execute harvesting when losses exist — waiting until the last day risks the position recovering and the loss disappearing.

### 2. Ignoring DeFi Losses

DeFi positions (LP tokens, liquidated loans, rug-pulled tokens) often represent real capital losses. These are often overlooked but qualify as capital losses. Identify worthless tokens and sell them (even for $0.01) to realize the loss.

### 3. Harvesting Without Tax Software

Without accurate cost basis tracking, you may unknowingly harvest a position that is actually at a gain (creating a taxable event rather than offsetting one). Always verify cost basis in tax software first.

### 4. Not Accounting for Gas Costs

On Ethereum mainnet, a harvesting transaction costs gas. If your unrealized loss is $200 and gas costs $50, the net benefit is modest. Use L2s where gas is $0.01–$0.10 for small positions.

### 5. Harvesting Losses You Plan to Hold Forever

If you harvest a loss but plan to hold the repurchased asset forever (or until death in a jurisdiction with step-up in basis), the tax savings are immediate but future gains on the new basis are deferred, not eliminated. Understand the long-term picture.

Tax-loss harvesting is entirely legal — it is the deliberate use of losses that are already occurring in your portfolio to reduce your current tax liability. In a volatile asset class like crypto, losses are almost always available for someone holding a diversified portfolio. Using them is simply good financial planning.
