Real World Assets have emerged as one of the most significant DeFi narratives of the mid-2020s. By 2026, tokenized U.S. Treasuries alone represent over $10 billion in onchain value — bringing the stability and yield of traditional financial instruments to DeFi's composable infrastructure.

RWAs bridge the gap between TradFi and DeFi, offering regulated, real-economy returns to crypto-native users while opening DeFi capital markets to traditional assets. This guide explains how it works, who the leading players are, and what risks remain. Stay current with RWA developments at [Crypto Vision News](/).

## What Are Real World Assets?

Real World Assets (RWAs) are tokenized representations of traditional financial instruments or physical assets:

- U.S. Treasury bonds and money market funds
- Corporate and government bonds
- Real estate
- Private credit and trade finance
- Commodities (gold, oil)
- Equities and funds

Tokenization means creating a blockchain-based token that represents a legal claim on the underlying asset. The token can then be traded, used as collateral, or integrated into DeFi protocols while the underlying asset is held in a regulated custodian or legal structure.

## Why RWAs Matter for DeFi

Traditional DeFi yield comes from crypto market activity — trading fees, borrowing demand, protocol token emissions. These yields are volatile and often correlated with crypto prices.

RWAs provide:
- **Uncorrelated yield:** Treasury yields exist regardless of crypto market conditions
- **Capital efficiency:** DeFi protocols can earn real-world returns on idle capital
- **Institutional adoption:** Regulated assets attract institutions that cannot hold purely crypto-native instruments
- **Stablecoin backing:** RWAs provide high-quality backing for algorithmic and overcollateralized stablecoins

When crypto markets are bearish and DeFi yields collapse to near zero, tokenized Treasury yields (currently 4–5%) become extremely attractive.

## Tokenized U.S. Treasuries

### Ondo Finance

Ondo is the market leader in tokenized short-duration Treasuries. Their flagship products:

- **OUSG:** Tokenized U.S. short-term Treasury ETF, targeting institutional users
- **USDY:** A yield-bearing USD note backed by Treasuries, accessible to non-U.S. retail investors
- **ondo.finance** provides a compliant, KYC-gated frontend

Yield: ~4.5–5% APY (tracks Fed funds rate)
Minimum: $5,000 for OUSG; lower for USDY

### Superstate

Founded by Compound Finance's Robert Leshner, Superstate operates a money market fund registered with the SEC and issues tokenized shares (USTB) on Ethereum. The structure provides traditional fund-level regulatory protections.

### BlackRock BUIDL

BlackRock's tokenized money market fund launched on Ethereum in 2024 and rapidly became one of the largest tokenized funds globally. BUIDL tokens represent shares in a fund holding cash and Treasury instruments, accessible to qualified investors via platforms like Securitize.

### Franklin Templeton BENJI

Franklin Templeton's BENJI fund operates on Stellar and Polygon, representing one of the first registered funds to record share ownership on a public blockchain.

## MakerDAO's RWA Strategy

MakerDAO (now Sky) led the way in DeFi protocol RWA adoption. The protocol allocated billions of DAI reserves into:

- Tokenized Treasury ETFs via Coinbase Custody
- Direct lending facilities (Huntingdon Valley Bank credit facility)
- Short-term Treasury bond investments via MonetaGo and others

By 2026, RWA backing represents approximately 40–60% of Sky's (formerly DAI's) collateral. This dramatically improved yield for MKR/SKY holders while introducing counterparty and regulatory risk.

## Real Estate Tokenization

Real estate tokenization fragments property ownership into tradeable tokens, enabling fractional investment in properties that previously required institutional capital.

**Key platforms:**

- **RealT:** U.S. rental properties tokenized as ERC-20 tokens; holders receive daily rental income
- **Tangible:** Real estate-backed stablecoin (USDR) and tokenized UK properties
- **Lofty.ai:** Algorand-based tokenized rental properties with $50 minimum investment

**Benefits:** Access to real estate cash flows without traditional barriers; 24/7 liquidity on secondary markets.

**Risks:** Legal complexity varies by jurisdiction; liquidity is thin compared to crypto-native assets; property management risk remains.

## Private Credit and Trade Finance

Platforms like Maple Finance, Centrifuge, and TrueFi enable onchain lending to real-world businesses — bridging DeFi liquidity to traditional credit markets.

**Maple Finance:** Institutional lending pools where DeFi capital flows to crypto-native businesses and fintech firms. Pools earn 8–15% yields but carry borrower default risk. Maple suffered defaults in the 2022 bear market, highlighting the risk.

**Centrifuge:** Tokenizes invoices, trade receivables, and structured credit products. The protocol pools these assets into DeFi-accessible tokens.

**Credix:** Private credit in emerging markets — Latin American fintech companies borrow from USDC liquidity pools.

## Benefits and Risks Summary

### Benefits

| Benefit | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| Stable yield | Uncorrelated with crypto market cycles |
| Diversification | Non-crypto income streams |
| Capital efficiency | Idle DeFi capital earning real-world returns |
| Institutional bridge | Compliant entry point for institutions |

### Risks

| Risk | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| Counterparty risk | Custodians, fund managers, legal entities can fail |
| Regulatory risk | Regulatory changes can freeze redemptions |
| Liquidity risk | Onchain markets for RWAs can be thin |
| Oracle dependency | Price feeds must accurately reflect underlying value |
| Centralization | Most RWA issuance relies on centralized entities |

## How to Access RWA Yields

1. **Ondo Finance (USDY):** Non-U.S. users can access 4–5% Treasury yields; no minimum beyond gas fees
2. **Superstate USTB:** KYC required; suitable for institutional DeFi participants
3. **DeFi protocols with RWA allocation:** Holding sDAI (Sky savings rate) passively exposes you to MakerDAO's RWA yields
4. **Pendle Finance:** Purchase fixed-rate OUSG or USDY yield for locked, predictable returns

RWAs represent the most significant maturation of DeFi since the launch of lending protocols. As tokenization standards improve and regulatory clarity increases, the RWA market is projected to reach hundreds of billions by the end of the decade.
